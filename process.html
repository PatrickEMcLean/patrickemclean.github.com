Why a Focus on Blog Posts?

Regular writing is the foundation of all content marketing. The greatest brands, companies and creators are not a singular event, but a stream of content that consistently delights.

In today's fast, iterative business-environment, the best place to start almost everything is a blog post.

Our Content Principles

You know a person by what they stand for. Here's what we believe about content.

First, be Helpful. There is no defense against co-operation. If you help people get what they want they will like you and buy from you.
Shorter is Better. Even people who want to connect with you are out of time. Just like any other craft, the skilled writer can get more done with less.
Write for a Person, Not a Target. We all crave real, human connection. If you think of your audience as things to be manipulated your writing will be bad and no one will like you.
The Best Ideas Deserve the Best Articulation. Not all good ideas are easy to explain. In fact, revolutionary ideas seldom are. We make certain that your message is presented as quickly and powerfully as possible.
Blog First. Everything else falls out of regular thoughtful blogging activity. Newsletters, ebooks, videos, new products, new insights, more effective strategies -- all of it is best and most naturally started on a blog.

Our Process

Fast, creative and collaborative.

You have the knowledge, but you are too close to it. You can write, but you're not fast enough, or it's not fun for you. Whatever the case, in an economic sense, your time is simply better spent on things other than writing.

We've got 10,000+ hours writing and developing content. Our process ensures that our expertise and passion are harnessed for you.

1. Initial Review

We start by gathering some basic information on your site and your business. Then, after a review of your current content and the content available in the space, we attempt to complete a Content Brief. Of course, we don't know everything we need to yet, that's why, there are more steps.

2. Consultation

We firm up the content brief and brainstorm ideas for our content calender. While the foundation we lay here will always be subject to revision, by the end of this meeting we will have a plan and be ready to start generating content.

3. Content Calendar

After we understand who we are creating content for and what we have to say to them, we will build out a schedule of what we are going to write and when it needs to be done.

4. Iterate

Each cycle we go through the following steps.

  1. Review results from last post.
  2. Confirm/generate topic for current post.
  3. Generate draft
  4. Incorporate changes, suggestions and concerns
  5. Draft Approval
  6. Proofing
  7. Posting
